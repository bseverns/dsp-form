# Privacy, Ethics, and Source Material

DSP Form Lab can turn recordings into objects. That makes source material important.

## Consent

Use recordings you made, recordings you have permission to use, or recordings with clear licensing terms.

If voices are present, treat the mesh as derived from a person’s presence, not as neutral data.

## Attribution

Keep source pointers in the manifest when possible:

- file name
- creator
- license
- URL or archive note
- transformation notes

## Minimization

For teaching, use short excerpts and synthetic test sounds first.

## Classroom safety

Good classroom source materials:

- sine waves
- noise bursts
- claps
- classroom-made percussion
- short student-created sound effects
- public-domain or openly licensed samples with clear attribution

Avoid casual use of private conversations, identifiable student voices, or sensitive recordings.

## Material honesty

Do not present a generated object as “the sound itself.” It is a mapping, a translation, a designed interpretation.
